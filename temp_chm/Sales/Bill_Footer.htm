Bill Round off

How to reach: Catalogue > Sales Factors > Add

The Sales Factors Catalogue window is displayed.

Record No: Enter a unique number to identify the sales factor.

Description: Enter an identification name for the sales factor.

Factor Type: Select Bill Round-Off from the drop down list.

Factor Category: Select the appropriate sales factor category from the drop down list.

The Customer Details are mandatory, if the selected Factor Category is customer specific.

Value: Enter the maximum value for rounding off ( e.g., 1).

Ok: Click to save the sales factor.

Cancel: Click to clear the values entered.

 

Bill Footer

The footer part of a bill has various components as explained below.

The total sales value appears in a grid at the bottom right corner (below Direct Entry grid) of the billing window. Click the button beside Sales Value to display the details.

The total sales value, discounts, sales tax (if configured to be printed), add-ons and deductions (if configured to be captured), and their respective Net Values of the billing made are displayed in this grid.

Sales Value: The net value of the total sales is displayed.

Discounts: The total discount value, if applicable, is displayed. Click the button beside Discounts in the grid or press F6, if any Item level/ Bill level promotional details are to be viewed. Press Esc to come out of the pop up.

Sales Tax: The total sales tax is displayed.

Click the button beside Sales Tax in the grid and the Sales Tax details pop up.

The Add-ons/ Deductions, if any, the total sales tax varies according to the type of computation defined in their respective sales factors (Add-on/ Deduction).

The billing where tax on Add-ons and Deductions are not considered for the calculation of total Sales Tax is displayed.

The billing where tax on Add-ons and Deductions are considered for the calculation of total Sales Tax is displayed.

F12: To suspend a bill. Depending on the parameter setting, the reference number for the suspended bill is entered or generated automatically.

Alt+M: To change the customer at any stage of billing.

The Bill Totals grid is displayed at the bottom part of the billing window.

Each field is explained below.

Total No. of Items: The total number of line items in the Item Details grid is displayed.  

Total Qty.: The total quantity of items selected for billing is displayed.

Sales Value: The total sale value of the selected items is displayed.

Item Level Discount: The total of item level discounts is displayed.

Bill Discount: The total of bill level discounts (item level discounts are not included) is displayed.

Note: Bill level discount value is appropriated against all the items selected for billing.

Total Tax: The total sales tax is displayed. The Total Tax is calculated as follows.

Total Tax = Total Sales Tax on items (both Tax inclusive items and Tax exclusive items) + Tax on Add-ons

Total Addons: The total value of add-ons is displayed.

Total Deductions: The total value of deductions is displayed.

Net Amount: The final amount to be paid by the customer is displayed. The Net Amount is calculated as follows.

Net Amount = (Total Sales value + Total Add-ons + Total Sales Tax) - (Total Discounts + Total Deductions)

The Total Discounts is the sum of Total Item level discounts & Bill level discount.

Net Amount is calculated by rounding off the calculated value to its nearest integer value, if Bill round off is catalogued. Click here to know how to catalogue Bill round off.

F7: To make bill settlement by cash. You can also click the button Exact Cash in the toolbar to make cash payment.

F8: To make bill settlement by using different Payment modes. You can also click the button Settlement in the toolbar to get the Mode of Payment grid.

 

Note: If a Sales Order/ Service Order with an advance payment is recalled during billing, the bill settlement cannot be done by using the option F7/ Exact Cash. If you try to settle the bill using the option Exact Cash, you are alerted with a message, Advance payment details found, Press F8 key to settle the bill.
