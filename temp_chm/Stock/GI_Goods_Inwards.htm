Goods Inwards

This option leads to the Goods Inwards form. The Goods Inwards form allows recording and updating of goods receipts. The goods receipts can be accounted in many ways. The details of inwards document and the physical quantity of the items received can be directly keyed in using this form. Goods can be received against the purchase order by providing PO reference so that the document quantity and item details will be directly picked from the PO available in the system. The other ways of goods inwards are through a PT and PDT files.

How to reach: Stock > Goods Inwards

In a chain store environment, PT Files received from the sender of the items, generally distribution center, can be provided as reference to avoid data entry of document details of the items. To record the quantity of items received physically, Shoper can either read the information from PDT file or from the internal document created during a process called Physical Verification wherein the items received are scanned by providing the reference of the Inwards document. The various ways explained here are optional and the user can opt for a convenient way of his choice.

Through this form, it is possible to record the details of stock where there is an inward movement or in other words whenever stock is received. You can record the goods received either through Purchase or Transfer from other locations.

To enter the values in the Goods Receipt screen, catalogues like Vendor, Item Classification, Items Master Entry must be defined.

Goods Inward can be based on Stock No. or based on Size-wise.

Extension frame work provided from Release 1.3 of Shoper 9 gives a certain degree of customisation in Goods Inwards.
