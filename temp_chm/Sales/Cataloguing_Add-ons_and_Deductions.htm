Cataloguing Add-ons and Deductions

The Sales Factors Catalogue window is displayed when you select Add at Catalogue > Sales Factors.

Record No: Enter a unique number to identify the sales factor.

Description: Enter an identification name for the sales factor.

Factor Type: Select Add-on or Deduction, as per the requirement, from the drop down list.

Factor Category: Select the appropriate sales factor category from the drop down list. The options available are Customer specific general, Customer Price Group specific and general and All Customers And General.

The Customer Details are mandatory, if the selected Factor Category is customer specific.

Application Details

Computation: Select the required tax condition, for the computation of the sales factor is to be applied, from the drop down list. The options available are:

  • Consider for Tax Calc.: The sales factor to be applied is considered for tax calculation.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax inclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Net amount = 1000(MRP) + 100 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of MRP) + 4 (4% of the Add-on value) = 1104.

If a deduction of 5% is added in this scenario,

Net amount = 1000(MRP) - 50 (Deduction, i.e., 5% of MRP) + 100 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of MRP)  + 4 (4% of the Add-on value) = 1054.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax exclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Net amount = 1000 (item rate) + 40 (4% tax on item rate) + 100 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of item rate) + 4 (4% of the Add-on value) = 1144.

If a deduction of 5% is added in this scenario,

Net amount = 1000 (item rate) + 40 (4% tax on item rate) - 50 (Deduction, i.e., 5% of item rate) + 100 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of item rate) + 4 (4% of the Add-on value) = 1094.

  • Ignore for Tax Calc.: The sales factor to be applied is not considered for tax calculation.

Computed On: Select the value, on which the sales factor is be calculated, from the drop down list. The options available are:

  • Sale Value Before Discount: The sales factor is calculated on the sale value before deducting discounts, if any.

In the case of Tax inclusive items, the sale value is calculated as Item MRP - Tax.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax inclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10%, the discount is 5% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Sale value = 1000 (MRP) - 38.46 (Tax value ) = 961.54.

Net amount = 961.54 (sale value) + 96.15 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of sale value) - 50 (discount, i.e., 5% of MRP) = 1007.69.

Note: The Tax value on each sales factor is to be added to the Net amount, if Consider for Tax Calc. is selected in Computation field.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax exclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10%, the discount is 5% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Sale value = 1000.

Net amount = 1000 (sale value) + 100 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of sale value) - 50 (discount, i.e., 5% of sale value) = 1050.

  • Discounted Sale Value: The sales factor is calculated on the sale value after deducting discounts, if any.

In the case of Tax inclusive items, the discounted sale value is calculated as Item MRP - Tax - Discount.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax inclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10%, the discount is 5% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Discounted sale value = 1000 (MRP) - 38.46 (Tax value ) - 50 (discount, i.e., 5% of MRP) = 911.54.

Net amount = 911.54 (Discounted sale value) + 91.15 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of discounted sale value) = 1002.69.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax exclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10%, the discount is 5% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Discounted sale value = 1000 (item rate) - 50 (discount, i.e., 5% of item rate) = 950.

Net amount = 950 (Discounted sale value) + 95 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of Discounted sale value) = 1045.

  • Value Inclusive of Tax: The sales factor is calculated on the Item MRP.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax inclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10%, the discount is 5% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Net amount = 1000 (MRP) + 100 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of MRP) - 50 (discount, i.e., 5% of MRP) = 1050.

An example to show the calculation of net amount in the case of Tax exclusive items is given.

Suppose the price of the item is Rs. 1000/-, the add-on rate is 10%, the discount is 5% and the tax is at 4%, then the net amount is calculated as follows.

Item MRP = 1000 (Item rate) + 40 (4% of Item rate) = 1040.

Net amount = 1040 (MRP) + 104 (Add-on value, i.e., 10% of MRP) - 50 (discount, i.e., 5% of Item rate) = 1094.

Variable Y/N: Select if the sales factor is of the type variable.

Rate: Select if a particular percentage of the cost is considered as the sales factor.

Amount: Select if a particular amount is considered as the sales factor.

Value: Enter the percentage or amount as per the selection.

Ok: To save the sales factor

Cancel: To clear the values entered
